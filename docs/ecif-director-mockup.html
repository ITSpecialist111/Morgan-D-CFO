Morgan — ECIF Director

Morgan is an autonomous AI agent built on the Microsoft CorpGen Autonomous Harness — a blueprint for digital workers that defines how an agent holds a persistent identity, plans and executes bounded work cycles, escalates through governed approval gates, and preserves auditable evidence of every action. Applied here to the full End Customer Investment Funds (ECIF) lifecycle: from identifying the right investment vehicle, through nomination pack assembly, approval chasing, SOW execution, POE evidence collection, payment processing, and case closeout.

Morgan wraps — not replaces — existing Microsoft systems (MSX, OneAsk, Partner Center, SharePoint) and operates with human-in-the-loop gates on every external communication and dollar-bearing decision. The sponsor seller remains in command; Morgan is the sponsor's hands.

Operating model: Built on the CorpGen Autonomous Harness — case-driven work cycles with HITL gates at L1 (info send), L2 (external comms), and L3 (dollar-bearing actions). The first agent took months to build; the blueprint means the next one takes hours. Evidence capture on every state transition. Wrap-not-replace existing Microsoft tooling.

Assumptions & Reasoning

Manual effort today (assumed): ~12h per ECIF case across the lifecycle — chasing approvers, gathering evidence, assembling packs, tracking deadlines, writing status updates. Based on seller self-reporting, not time-tracked data.

Agent-assisted effort (target): ~1h per case — sponsor reviews HITL cards, approves sends, reviews digests. The agent handles chasing, evidence capture, pack assembly, and deadline monitoring autonomously.

Forfeited claim risk (assumed): 3–5 cases/year at risk of missed POE deadlines due to late evidence gathering. At $100K–$500K per case, this represents $300K–$2.5M in preventable loss. Continuous evidence capture is designed to eliminate this.

Approval acceleration (hypothesis): Automated nudge cadence (soft → firm → escalation → call) should reduce approval wait times by ~25%. Not yet validated.
