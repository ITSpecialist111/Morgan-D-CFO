# Morgan Digital CFO - Microsoft FTE Pitch

## Positioning

Morgan is a working showcase of what happens when we stop thinking about AI as chat and start thinking about AI as an enterprise worker.

Morgan is a Digital CFO built around CorpGen, an operating model for autonomous digital workers. She has a role, a schedule, a task board, memory, tools, governance, audit, cost visibility, and a daily operating rhythm. She works from 9 to 5, seven days a week, monitoring financial health, generating executive briefings, escalating risks, calling Microsoft IQ-style business signals, and producing proof of work at the end of every day.

The reason this matters for Microsoft is that Morgan is not just an agent demo. Morgan is a Microsoft stack story.

## Core Pitch

Most enterprise AI demos still look like chat. They answer a question, summarize a document, or retrieve a piece of information. That is useful, but it is not how work actually gets done in a business.

Work has roles. Work has accountability. Work has systems. Work has handoffs. Work has cost. Work has audit. Work has blocked items. Work has daily rhythms.

Morgan shows what an AI agent becomes when it is treated as a governed digital employee rather than a chat surface.

Morgan is the Digital CFO for an enterprise. She understands her job description, runs her daily operating loop, checks finance health, monitors anomalies, prepares executive briefings, manages her own autonomous Kanban, escalates issues to humans, and writes an end-of-day report showing what she completed, what was blocked, and where attention is needed.

This is powered by CorpGen, an operating model for enterprise digital workers. CorpGen gives Morgan the things a serious enterprise agent needs: planning, task execution, memory, specialist sub-agents, cognitive tools, governance, evidence, cost tracking, and human escalation.

For Microsoft, the important point is that Morgan brings the Microsoft platform together into one coherent enterprise story.

Morgan shows how Agent 365 can become the control plane for digital workers. She uses Microsoft Graph and MCP-style tools to reason across work, people, meetings, documents, and actions. She uses Foundry IQ for model intelligence and agent orchestration. She uses Fabric IQ for financial and operational data. She uses WorkIQ to understand the flow of work across the organization. She uses Teams for escalation and human collaboration. She uses Azure for identity, hosting, observability, scheduling, cost tracking, and enterprise readiness. She exposes all of that through Mission Control, so leaders can see what the agent is doing, why it is doing it, what it costs, and where human approval is needed.

That is the real story: Morgan turns the Microsoft cloud into the operating environment for autonomous enterprise labor.

## Why This Matters

The CFO office is one of the best places to prove this category because the work is high-value, recurring, cross-functional, data-heavy, and trust-sensitive.

Finance teams do not need another static dashboard. They need an autonomous operator that can continuously turn enterprise signals into decisions, actions, and evidence.

Morgan can reduce manual finance operations, speed up reporting cycles, identify risks earlier, give executives daily visibility, and create an audit trail for every action. Instead of hiring more people to chase information across Microsoft 365, Fabric, Power BI, ERP, Teams, and finance systems, Morgan sits across that environment and works continuously.

This makes the Microsoft stack more valuable because Morgan is not a single-product demo. She is a workload that depends on the whole platform working together.

## Demo Narrative

1. Open Mission Control and show Morgan's role as Digital CFO.
2. Show the 9 to 5, seven-days-a-week autonomous operating model.
3. Show today's completed work, blocked items, and autonomous Kanban.
4. Show the CorpGen planning loop and how Morgan knows what to do without being prompted.
5. Show Microsoft IQ surfaces: WorkIQ, Foundry IQ, and Fabric IQ.
6. Show Graph and Agent 365-style tool visibility.
7. Show the Agent Mind or audit activity so the audience can see the agent's reasoning and actions.
8. Show the Cost of Morgan dashboard, including daily and weekly cost, estimated value, and platform cost categories.
9. Show Teams escalation or human handoff as the governance point.
10. End with the avatar experience: Morgan is not just a backend agent, she is a digital worker people can interact with.
